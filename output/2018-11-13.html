FPGA Competition
Date: Friday Dec. 7, 2018
Time: 17:30 - 19:00
Location: TBD (Carleton)

The Digital Electronics (ELEC 3500) course covers the fundamentals of logic gates, circuit timing, and design with HDL (Hardware Description Language) along with guiding the students to create a basic alarm project.

The FPGA competition will challenge students to modify their alarm project by creating new rules or functionalities. They are required to provide a block diagram describing their implementation. Students are strongly encouraged to work on their code prior to the competition because they will only have 1 hour before judging begins.

Participation is free, and pizza will be provided to all participants. Winners will be awarded with an IEEE certificate. Other prizes will be announced at a later date.

Follow us on facebook for more info.
